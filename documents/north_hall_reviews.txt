North Hall Dining — Student Reviews (r/college dining thread, Fall 2025)

Review by u/coffee_and_code: North Hall has the shortest lunch wait on campus if you go before 11:45. After noon the line wraps around the pasta station and you're looking at 20-25 minutes. The grilled chicken is consistently good; avoid the mystery casserole on Tuesdays.

Review by u/mealplan_sufferer: I live in the North quad and basically live here. Breakfast is underrated — the omelet bar opens at 7am and there's never a line before 8. Lunch rush is brutal though. Pro tip: use the side entrance near the gym, saves 5 minutes.

Review by u/vegan_on_campus: Vegan options at North are better than the official dining page suggests. There's always a grain bowl station and the salad bar is huge. They label allergens clearly, which South Hall does not.

Review by u/freshman2025: Compared to my high school cafeteria this is fine. Compared to what upperclassmen describe from five years ago, portions are smaller. The swipe system changed last year and you can't get seconds on entrees without paying extra.
